INDIAN PREMIER LEAGUE (IPL)

Match Update:

Chennai Super Kings(CSK) VS Mumbai Indians(MI)


On March 23, 2025, at the MA Chidambaram Stadium in Chennai, the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) secured a thrilling 4-wicket win over the Mumbai Indians (MI) in the IPL 2025. MI, batting first, struggled to build momentum and managed 155/9 in their 20 overs. CSK chased down the target with five balls to spare, finishing at 158/6 in 19.1 overs.

MI’s innings was derailed by CSK's sharp bowling, especially from Afghan spinner Noor Ahmad, who delivered a game-changing spell with figures of 4/18. He was well supported by Ravindra Jadeja and Ravichandran Ashwin, who kept the scoring rate in check and took key wickets at regular intervals. Despite a few late fireworks from MI’s lower order, the total looked below par on a Chennai pitch offering turn.

In response, CSK’s chase began steadily. Skipper Ruturaj Gaikwad anchored the innings with a composed 53, laying the foundation for a successful chase. The standout performer was Rachin Ravindra, who smashed an unbeaten 65, guiding CSK home calmly under pressure. His knock included timely boundaries and smart running between the wickets.


This victory marked CSK's third consecutive win over their arch-rivals MI, further solidifying their upper hand in this iconic IPL rivalry. With this win, CSK continues to look like a strong contender in the tournament, boasting both a balanced squad and winning momentum.

Royal Challengers Benguluru(RCB) VS Mumbai Indians(MI)


On April 7, 2025, at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai, Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) defeated Mumbai Indians (MI) by 12 runs in a high-scoring IPL 2025 match. RCB batted first and put up an impressive total of 221/5 in their 20 overs.

Rajat Patidar was the star with the bat, smashing 64 off just 32 balls and earning the Player of the Match award. Virat Kohli played a crucial knock of 51 runs, during which he reached the remarkable milestone of 13,000 T20 runs. Devdutt Padikkal added 35, while Glenn Maxwell contributed a quickfire 28, helping RCB build a challenging total.

In response, MI fought hard but fell short, ending their innings at 209/9. Tilak Varma anchored the chase with a fighting 65, and Hardik Pandya kept hopes alive with a fiery 45 off 20 balls. However, RCB’s bowlers held their nerve in the final overs to secure a 12-run win.


The victory pushed RCB to their fourth win in six games, taking them to third place on the IPL 2025 points table. Meanwhile, MI suffered their fourth loss in six matches, slipping to seventh place. The match was a showcase of RCB's explosive batting and their ability to defend big totals under pressure, giving them a valuable edge as the tournament progresses.

Royal Challengers Benguluru(RCB) VS Chennai Super Kings(CSK)


On March 28, 2025, at the MA Chidambaram Stadium in Chennai, Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) defeated Chennai Super Kings (CSK) by 50 runs in a dominant IPL 2025 performance. RCB set a strong foundation by scoring 196/7 in their 20 overs. Rajat Patidar starred with the bat, smashing a quick 78 off 45 balls. He was well supported by Faf du Plessis, who scored 38, and Glenn Maxwell, who added 32 runs to help RCB post a challenging total.

CSK's bowlers found it difficult to contain the aggressive RCB batting lineup, and their fielding lacked intensity, allowing RCB to capitalize on key moments.

In the chase, CSK never found momentum, losing early wickets and struggling to build partnerships. Although MS Dhoni tried to revive the innings with a fighting 30 off 16 balls, it wasn’t enough to turn the tide. CSK eventually ended at 146/8 in their 20 overs, falling well short of the target.

RCB’s bowlers executed their plans effectively, maintaining pressure throughout and never letting CSK into the game. This win was a historic one for RCB as it marked their first victory at Chepauk since 2008.


After the match, CSK coach Stephen Fleming admitted the team is struggling to adjust to the changing nature of the home pitch, noting that what used to be their stronghold is now posing challenges due to unpredictable surface behavior. The loss puts CSK in a reflective mode as they look to bounce back in the tournament.

Kolkata Knight Riders(KKR) VS Mumbai Indians(MI)


On March 31, 2025, at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai, the Mumbai Indians (MI) registered a dominant 8-wicket victory over the Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) in Match 12 of the IPL 2025 season. This was MI's first win of the season, ending their early losing streak and bringing much-needed momentum to their campaign.

Batting first, KKR struggled against MI’s sharp bowling attack and were bowled out for just 116 runs in 16.2 overs. Andre Russell was the top scorer with a modest 24 runs, as the rest of the batting lineup failed to build any significant partnerships. MI’s bowlers were clinical, with Jasprit Bumrah and Piyush Chawla leading the way by consistently taking wickets and not allowing KKR to settle.

In reply, MI chased down the target with ease, reaching 121/2 in just 12.5 overs. Rohit Sharma was in sublime form, remaining unbeaten on 65 and anchoring the innings with calm authority. Ishan Kishan also played a key role, scoring a quick 40 to keep the momentum in MI’s favor.


The comprehensive win not only earned MI two valuable points but also significantly boosted their net run rate. It was a much-needed confidence booster for the team after a shaky start. On the other hand, KKR will need to address their batting issues and regroup quickly if they are to remain competitive in the tournament.

Kings Eleven Punjab(PBKS) VS Rajasthan Royals(RR)


On April 5, 2025, at the Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium in Mullanpur, Rajasthan Royals (RR) defeated Punjab Kings (PBKS) by 50 runs in a dominant display during Match 18 of IPL 2025.

Batting first, RR posted an imposing total of 205/4 in their 20 overs. Yashasvi Jaiswal was the standout performer with a fluent 67 off 45 balls, striking five sixes and three fours. Captain Sanju Samson added a quick 38, while Riyan Parag provided a strong finish with an unbeaten 43, helping RR reach their highest score of the season.

In reply, PBKS struggled against a fiery bowling attack from Rajasthan. Jofra Archer struck early, taking two wickets in his first over, including that of PBKS captain Shreyas Iyer. Archer’s pace and accuracy dismantled the top order and set the tone for the defense. Despite a fighting 88-run partnership between Nehal Wadhera (62) and Glenn Maxwell (30), Punjab couldn’t keep up with the required run rate and finished on 155/9.

Archer’s impressive performance earned him the Player of the Match award, a remarkable comeback after a slow start to the season. PBKS skipper Shreyas Iyer admitted the team lacked execution and needed to improve in key areas moving forward.


This win was RR’s second in a row, strengthening their position in the points table. For PBKS, it was a wake-up call as they continue searching for consistency in both batting and bowling departments.
